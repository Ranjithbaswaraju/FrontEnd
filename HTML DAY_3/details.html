HERE IS INFORMATION ABOUT LEGENDS OF INDIAN CRICKET

ROHIT SHARMA

CAPTAIN ROHIT SHARMA(HIT-MAN)

Rohit Sharma, born on April 30, 1987, in Bansod, Nagpur, India, showed early promise in cricket. Here's a detailed overview of Rohit Sharma's life and career, starting from his childhood

Childhood and Early Life:

Rohit Sharma was born into a humble family in Bansod, Nagpur, Maharashtra, India. His father, Gurunath Sharma, worked as a caretaker of a transportation company storehouse, while his mother, Purnima Sharma, managed the household. Rohit grew up with his younger brother, Vishal Sharma, in a middle-class neighborhood. From a young age, Rohit showed a keen interest in cricket. He often played cricket with his friends in the streets and local playgrounds. Recognizing his talent, Rohit's father encouraged him to pursue cricket seriously and provided unwavering support throughout his journey.

International Debut:

Rohit Sharma made his international debut for India in both One Day Internationals (ODIs) and Twenty20 Internationals (T20Is) in 2007. He showcased glimpses of his talent early in his career but struggled to establish himself as a permanent member of the Indian team.

ACHIEVEMENTS

1.Highest Individual Score in ODIs: Rohit Sharma holds the record for the highest individual score in One Day Internationals (ODIs), with an astonishing 264 runs against Sri Lanka in 2014.
2.Three Double Centuries in ODIs: He is the only cricketer to score three double centuries in ODIs, showcasing his remarkable batting prowess.
3.Fastest T20I Century by an Indian: Rohit Sharma holds the record for the fastest century in Twenty20 Internationals (T20Is) by an Indian batsman, achieving it off just 35 balls against Sri Lanka in 2017.
4.ICC Champions Trophy Triumphs: He has been part of the Indian team that won the ICC Champions Trophy twice, in 2013 and 2018.
5.Successful IPL Captaincy: As captain of the Mumbai Indians franchise in the Indian Premier League (IPL), Rohit Sharma has led the team to multiple IPL titles, establishing himself as one of the most successful captains in IPL history.
6.Honors and Awards: Rohit Sharma has received prestigious awards such as the Arjuna Award and the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna, recognizing his outstanding contributions to cricket.

These achievements highlight Rohit Sharma's exceptional talent, consistency, and leadership qualities, making him one of the most celebrated cricketers in the world.

VIRAT KOHLI

RUN MACHINE

Virat Kohli was born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, to Prem Kohli and Saroj Kohli. He grew up in a middle-class family in West Delhi, where his father worked as a criminal lawyer and his mother was a homemaker. Virat developed a keen interest in cricket from a young age and started playing the sport at the age of three. His father supported his passion for cricket and enrolled him in the West Delhi Cricket Academy.

Early Life and Introduction to Cricket:

Virat Kohli grew up in a middle-class family in West Delhi. His father, Prem Kohli, supported his passion for cricket from a young age. Virat started playing cricket at the age of three and received initial coaching at the West Delhi Cricket Academy.

International debut and success:

Virat Kohli made his international debut for India in 2008 in a One Day International (ODI) against Sri Lanka. He soon established himself as a mainstay in the Indian team with his remarkable batting talent and hunger for success.

ACHIVEMENTS

1.Fastest to 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, and 11,000 Runs in ODIs: Virat Kohli holds the records for being the fastest batsman to reach 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, and 11,000 runs in One Day Internationals (ODIs).
2.Fastest Century by an Indian in ODIs: Kohli holds the record for the fastest century by an Indian cricketer in ODIs, achieving the milestone off 52 balls against Australia in 2013.
3.ICC Cricketer of the Year: He has been awarded the prestigious Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for ICC Cricketer of the Year multiple times.
4.Leading Run-Scorer in IPL: Kohli is one of the leading run-scorers in the history of the Indian Premier League (IPL). He holds several records in the IPL, including the most runs scored in a single season.
5.Arjuna Award: He was conferred with the Arjuna Award by the Government of India in recognition of his outstanding achievements in cricket.
6.Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna: Kohli has been honored with the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna, India's highest sporting honor, for his exceptional contributions to cricket.
7.ICC World Cup: He played a crucial role in India's victory in the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup and has been a key player in subsequent World Cup campaigns.
8.Test Cricket Records: Kohli holds numerous records in Test cricket, including being the fastest to score 25 centuries in terms of innings played.

These achievements highlight Virat Kohli's exceptional talent, consistency, and contributions to Indian cricket, making him one of the greatest cricketers of all time.

MS DHONI

CAPTAIN COOL

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, widely known as MS Dhoni or Mahi, was born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, Jharkhand, India.MS Dhoni was born into a middle-class Rajput family in Ranchi. His father, Pan Singh, worked as a junior manager in MECON, a public sector undertaking, while his mother, Devaki Devi, was a housewife. Dhoni has a sister, Jayanti Gupta, and a brother, Narendra Singh Dhoni.

Introduction to Cricket:

From a young age, Dhoni showed a keen interest in sports, particularly cricket and football. He initially played as a goalkeeper in football before focusing solely on cricket. His love for cricket led him to spend countless hours practicing the sport in the streets and local grounds of Ranchi.

Struggles and Determination:

Dhoni's journey in cricket was not without its challenges. Coming from a modest background, he faced financial constraints and had to overcome various obstacles to pursue his passion for cricket. However, his determination and dedication never wavered, and he continued to work hard towards his goal of playing cricket at the highest level.

Rise in Junior Cricket:

Dhoni's talent soon caught the attention of local coaches and selectors. He began playing in local cricket tournaments and quickly made a name for himself with his powerful batting and wicket-keeping skills. He represented his school and district teams in various age-group competitions, laying the foundation for his future success.

ACHIVEMENTS

1.ICC Trophies: Dhoni is the only captain to have led his team to victory in all three major ICC tournaments - ICC Cricket World Cup (2007), ICC World Twenty20 (2007), and ICC Champions Trophy (2013).
2.World Cup Glory: He captained the Indian cricket team to victory in the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, ending a 28-year drought for India.
3.ICC Player of the Year: Dhoni was named the ICC ODI Player of the Year in 2008 and the ICC Spirit of Cricket Award in 2011.
4.Leading Run-Scorer as Wicketkeeper: Dhoni holds the record for the most runs scored by a wicketkeeper-batsman in ODIs, surpassing 10,000 runs.
5.IPL Success: He captained the Chennai Super Kings (CSK) to three Indian Premier League (IPL) titles in 2010, 2011, and 2018.
6.Padma Bhushan: Dhoni was awarded the Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civilian award, in 2018 for his contributions to Indian cricket.
7.Test Cricket Records: He holds several records in Test cricket, including the most dismissals by an Indian wicketkeeper.
8.Captaincy Records: Dhoni is one of the most successful captains in the history of Indian cricket, with numerous series wins and a remarkable win percentage across all formats.

These achievements highlight Dhoni's exceptional leadership, batting prowess, and contributions to Indian cricket, making him one of the most iconic figures in the history of the sport.
